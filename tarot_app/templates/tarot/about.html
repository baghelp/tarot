About Tarot

It should be simple to make a good financial plan for your future. Tarot is a tool that walks you through making a simple financial plan. It intentionally imposes some good habits on your financial plans. It assumes that your income is used:

  1. First, to save up some "emergency" cash in your savings account
  2. Second, to contribute to your retirement
  3. Third, the rest is invested in some other way
I hope this pattern is simple enough that it doesn't scare away newcomers. I like that it specifically introduces investing as part of the plan, since I have struggled for years to convince my family that investing can be good. Finally, it is still general enough to handle many people's plans -- indeed, I'm using Tarot for my own planning.

If the above seems crazy to you, I would encourage you to read "The Investment Answer" by Daniel Goldie, which largely summarizes my understanding of investing.
These are some features that I would like to include:
  • include suggestions / tips for common investments ("eg, over the long term, the SP500 returns roughly 8 percent yearly")
  • add description of how to plan for retirement
  • include withdrawals during retirement
  • include house purchase as its own invesment
  • users can sign up for email reminders every X months to revisit their progress towards their goals
  • explicitly handle taxes, instead of including it in expenses
  • include variance in the growth of investments
  • probably some others I've forgotten!
I don't know when (or if) I'll get to these. If you are very passionate about some feature, or have feedback of any kind, please let me know by emailing mbagherpour [the number five] at gmail. I especially want to hear your suggestions for how to simplify planning, saving, and investing for newcomers.
